What Does a NOC Engineer Do at a Finance Company?

A behind-the-scenes look at day-to-day network operations at People's Leasing and Finance — monitoring, incidents, and keeping the network alive 24/7.

When most people think about a finance company, they think of loans, investments, and customer service. But behind every transaction, every branch connection, and every online service is a network that has to work perfectly — all day, every day. That's where I come in as a Network Operations Center (NOC) Engineer.

What is a NOC?

A Network Operations Center is a centralized location where IT professionals monitor, manage, and maintain an organization's network infrastructure. At People's Leasing and Finance, the NOC is responsible for keeping connectivity stable across all branch networks and critical financial services.

My Day-to-Day Responsibilities

  • Monitoring enterprise network infrastructure in real time using Grafana and Itheon
  • Troubleshooting TCP/IP connectivity issues across branch networks
  • Responding to network incidents and minimizing downtime
  • Investigating outages and performing root cause analysis
  • Generating detailed incident reports for management

Tools I Use Every Day

The three main tools in my daily workflow are Grafana for network visualization and dashboards, Itheon for monitoring, and Fortinet for network security management.

Why NOC Work Matters in Finance

In a financial institution, network downtime is not just an inconvenience — it directly impacts customer transactions, branch operations, and regulatory compliance. Every second of downtime has real consequences, which makes the NOC role both challenging and critically important.
